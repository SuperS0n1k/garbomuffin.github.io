Why pay only 1 sat/byte?

Because why pay more if its not needed?

Obviously if your transaction needs to be in the next block then you'll have to pay a higher fee.

But most transactions that you do day to day aren't that important and you are probably okay with them taking even days to confirm.

The main reason fees are often so high is transaction spam. Here's what I mean:

That sudden gain of almost 14MB of transactions is totally not spam. Definitely organic growth. (/s if it wasn't implied)

You may notice in that chart that the transactions with 20-30 sat/byte fees are starting to get confirmed towards the end.

Given enough time eventually 1 sat/byte transactions will start to get mined again. Just like before the spam.

If you're paying 1 sat/byte you are very well aware of the downsides of this, namely slow confirmation times.

Best case scenario:

Transaction gets confirmed right away because the mempool is empty.

Worst case scenario:

Transaction gets dropped from mempool, you get your coins back.

The average is somewhere in between.

Just a reminder: If your transaction doesn't get confirmed after several days you get your coins back.
