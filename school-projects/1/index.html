A Journey Through Citizenship

By Thomas Weber -- For school [2nd hour civics]

How to become a citizen.

1. Get residency visa.

Using the I485 form.

If accepted:

- Right to live in the US permanently.

- Right to get a job.

- Right to be protected by all US laws.

- Responsibile to obey all laws.

2. Apply for citizenship.

Using the N400 form.

Must already be a US citizen for at least 5 years.
3 if married to a US citizen.

Get 2 photographs of yourself.

Pay a fee of $675.

3. Get fingerprinted.

If you got accepted:

- You will recieve a letter in the mail.
This could take several months.

- You will have to come to a doctor and have a background check.

4. Interview.

You will take a test about US history and the government.

You have 2 chances to get 85% or better

5. Oath of Allegiance

If you passed the test:

You will go to a naturalization ceremony where you will recieve your naturalization certificate.

And now you are an American citizen.

Rights you have as an American Citizen:

Freedom of speech.

The freedom to say whatever you want.

Freedom of Press.

Freedom of Religion.

The freedom to be whatever religion you want.

Right to a fair trial by jury.

Right to run for elected office.

Except president.
