Budgeting

Budgeting is part of the core of our economy. Without it, our government wouldn't provide all the things it does. Businesses wouldn't be able to stay alive. And your family might not even exist. That's why it's important and why you should learn about it.

What is budgeting?

Planning how to spend your money and make sure you're making more than you're spending.

When should you budget?

Always.

Examples of needs:
  • Food
  • Water
  • Shelter
  • etc
Examples of wants:
  • New phone
  • New clothes
  • Fidget spinners
  • etc
Who budgets?
  • The government
  • Companies
  • Your family
  • Everyone
How do you budget?

There are many ways you can budget:

  • Pen and paper
  • Excel/spreadsheets
  • Using an app
  • And many more

What is the process of budgeting?
  1. First, figure out how much money you will have to spend.
  2. Identify what you NEED to spend your money on. (needs)
  3. Figure out how much you need to spend in each of those categories, and allocate accordingly.
  4. Calculate how much money you will have after needs are paid for.
  5. If you don't have any money or have a negative amount you will have to find a way to cut expenses.
  6. If you have money left, then you should decide what you WANT to buy. (wants)
  7. Spend some of your remaining money on those categories.
  8. Remember to keep some extra!
Things to save for:
Planned
  • Retirement
  • Vacation
Unforseen
  • Natural disasters
  • The economy is doing poorly
  • You lose your job
